## Question

In 2022, Hans Moke Niemann faced public allegations of cheating in chess. Could a data-driven analysis of his games surface anything anomalous compared to peers?

## Approach

- **Data collection** via web scraping of public game databases.
- **Move-by-move evaluation** with **Stockfish 11** to compute an AI-match score for every move played.
- **Comparison set** of two reference players: Magnus Carlsen (the world's best at the time) and Vincent Keymer (a comparable rising player).
- **Interactive visualization** of the results in Microsoft Power BI dashboards.

## What the data showed

Niemann's measured playing strength was **not exceptional** compared to the reference players — yet his Elo rating climbed faster than either of them over the analyzed period, the most interesting anomaly the analysis surfaced.

## Stack

Python (web scraping, Stockfish 11 integration), Microsoft Power BI.
